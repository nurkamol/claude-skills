Why Sacramento Energy Bills Keep Climbing

Sacramento sits in one of California's most demanding climate zones. Summer temperatures regularly exceed 100°F from June through September, and the heat season often stretches into October. Running air conditioning for five to six months a year isn't a luxury in the Sacramento Valley — it's a basic requirement for livable indoor conditions.

PG&E's tiered rate structure compounds the problem. Under tiered pricing, every kilowatt-hour you consume beyond your baseline threshold costs more than the one before it. Homes with inefficient HVAC systems quickly push through those thresholds and end up paying the highest per-kWh rates for a large portion of their summer usage — turning a modestly inefficient system into a dramatically more expensive one than raw efficiency numbers would suggest.

The most frustrating part: most of this waste is invisible. Your thermostat doesn't tell you that 25% of your cooled air is escaping into the attic before it reaches your living room. Your utility bill shows the outcome — not the cause. That's precisely what a certified HERS evaluation is designed to reveal.

What a HERS Rating Actually Measures

HERS stands for Home Energy Rating System. Developed by RESNET (the Residential Energy Services Network) and required by California under Title 24, HERS is the official standardized method for measuring and comparing a home's energy performance against California's building energy standards.

Unlike a general home inspection, a HERS evaluation uses calibrated diagnostic equipment to measure the actual performance of your home's HVAC system and building envelope — not estimates, not design specifications, but real measured data. A certified HERS rater from Express Duct Test performs on-site field verification and delivers the completed report the same day, before leaving your property.

The tests typically cover duct leakage, refrigerant charge accuracy, airflow across the evaporator coil, air handler fan efficiency, and insulation installation quality. Each test produces documented, registerable results that can be submitted for permit compliance or used as a baseline for planning energy improvements.

7 Proven Ways a HERS Rating Cuts Your Energy Costs

1. Finds Duct Leakage — the Biggest Silent Budget Killer

Duct leakage is the single most common finding in Sacramento homes, and the most expensive one. California Energy Commission data shows the average Sacramento Valley home loses 20–30% of its conditioned air through duct leaks before it ever reaches the living space. That means roughly one-quarter of every dollar you spend on cooling is going straight into the attic or crawlspace.

A HERS duct leakage test pressurizes your duct system using a calibrated fan and measures precisely how much air escapes — expressed as a percentage of total system airflow. The result tells you and your HVAC contractor exactly how much leakage you have, whether it exceeds California's Title 24 thresholds, and what kind of improvement to expect after professional sealing.

For most Sacramento homeowners, duct sealing following a HERS test is the highest-return single investment they can make in their home's energy performance. Learn more about what the testing process reveals in our guide on uncovering hidden savings with duct testing.

2. Catches Refrigerant Charge Errors That Inflate Your Bill

Your air conditioner's refrigerant charge must stay within a very narrow range to operate at rated efficiency. A charge that's off by just 10% can increase your cooling costs noticeably, put additional strain on the compressor, and shorten equipment life by years. This issue is surprisingly common — many Sacramento systems were never properly commissioned at installation, and others have lost refrigerant over time without anyone noticing.

HERS refrigerant charge verification uses calibrated measurement protocols — not guesswork — to confirm your system is operating within specification. For many homeowners, a single refrigerant correction visit delivers a meaningful reduction in their next summer's cooling bills.

3. Identifies Airflow Restrictions Causing Long, Costly Cycles

When airflow across the evaporator coil is too low — due to a dirty filter, a kinked flex duct run, an undersized return duct, or a blocked coil — your system can't exchange heat efficiently. The result is longer run cycles that consume more electricity per degree of cooling delivered, and rooms that never quite reach the thermostat setpoint no matter how long the system runs.

HERS airflow measurement verifies that your system is actually moving the volume of air it's rated for. It's a direct check that reveals airflow problems many HVAC service visits overlook because they don't include measurement equipment.

4. Checks Fan Efficacy Against California's Title 24 Standards

California's 2019 and 2022 Title 24 code cycles introduced specific air handler fan efficiency requirements — measured in watts per CFM (cubic feet per minute). An oversized blower motor, or one operating outside the required efficiency range, adds to your electricity bill every single day it runs.

HERS fan efficacy verification confirms your air handler fan operates within California's required range. This is one of the measures that appears with increasing frequency on Sacramento permit CF-1R forms, and one that routine HVAC service visits typically don't check.

5. Reveals Insulation Gaps That Let Sacramento's Heat Pour In

Sacramento attics can reach 140–160°F during peak summer. Without adequate insulation between that superheated space and your living area, your air conditioner fights a continuous uphill battle — cooling air that the attic heat is actively warming back up. California's current code requires R-38 insulation in attics for many residential applications; older Sacramento homes frequently have R-13 or R-19 at best.

A HERS evaluation includes an assessment of your insulation levels and can reveal whether your home meets current California energy code standards — or is significantly underperforming and costing you money every day as a result.

6. Delivers a Prioritized, Data-Driven Upgrade Roadmap

One of the most valuable outputs of a Sacramento HERS rating is a ranked list of improvements ordered by energy-savings potential. Instead of guessing whether to replace windows, upgrade insulation, or install new HVAC equipment first, you get clear, measurement-based guidance. Every dollar you invest goes to the improvement with the highest documented return for your specific home — not a generic recommendation that may or may not apply to your situation.

This roadmap is especially valuable for Sacramento homeowners who have a budget for energy improvements and want to spend it where it will make the most difference before the next cooling season.

7. Unlocks PG&E Rebates and California Incentive Programs

Many of California's energy efficiency rebate programs — including PG&E's residential programs — require documented HERS testing to qualify. Getting a HERS evaluation before undertaking upgrades ensures you're eligible for every available rebate dollar, which can meaningfully offset the cost of improvements like duct sealing, new HVAC equipment, or attic insulation upgrades.

Without the HERS documentation, those rebates are simply unavailable — even if the work itself qualifies. Planning the HERS test before the upgrade work, rather than after, keeps all incentive options open. For current information on available California energy efficiency programs, the California Energy Commission's Building Standards program page is the authoritative resource.

Understanding Your HERS Rating Sacramento Score

A HERS rating is expressed as a numerical score. A score of 100 represents a home built to the 2006 energy code — essentially the baseline for a code-compliant home from that era. A score of 0 represents a net-zero home that produces as much energy as it consumes. The lower the score, the more energy efficient the home.

Most existing Sacramento homes without significant energy upgrades score between 100 and 150 — meaning they use substantially more energy than a modern code-compliant home. Homes that have completed HERS-guided improvements commonly score in the 60–80 range, representing real, measurable reductions in both energy consumption and monthly operating costs.

For comparison, a brand-new Sacramento home built to the current 2022 Title 24 code typically scores around 45–55. The gap between an older Sacramento home at 130 and a post-improvement home at 75 represents tens of thousands of kilowatt-hours per year in avoided energy consumption — and hundreds of dollars per year in lower utility bills.

How long does a HERS rating take?

For a typical Sacramento single-family home with one HVAC system, a complete HERS field verification visit takes 1.5 to 2.5 hours on-site. The report is completed and left at the property the same day — no waiting for documentation to arrive later.

Is a HERS rating required for all Sacramento HVAC replacements?

Not all replacements require it — the requirement is determined by the specific HERS measures listed on the CF-1R compliance form submitted with the permit. Full system replacements involving both the air handler and condenser almost always trigger HERS measures. Partial replacements may or may not, depending on what the CF-1R specifies.
